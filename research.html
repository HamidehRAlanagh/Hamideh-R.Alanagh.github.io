Bio-Inspired Coacervate Materials

We design stimuli-responsive protein–nucleic acid coacervates with tunable properties. Our work links molecular sequence design to phase behavior and function, enabling adaptive materials with controlled microenvironments and responsive transitions.

Mussel Foot Protein (MFP) Coacervates

Research Period: 2023-2024

Status: Published in Chemistry of Materials, 2025

This foundational work demonstrated how varying ionic conditions, specifically using multivalent sulfate ions, alters phase boundaries and increases coacervate stiffness and concentration. I measured these effects with optical tweezers microrheology and FRAP, which tracks molecular mobility inside the coacervate.

Key Techniques: Optical tweezers microrheology, FRAP
