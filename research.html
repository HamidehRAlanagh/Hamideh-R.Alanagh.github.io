About Me

I am an interdisciplinary scientist with a background in physical chemistry, biomaterials, and environmental chemistry. My work spans reticular chemistry, protein-based coacervates, and sustainable materials design. I am passionate about using fundamental chemistry to address challenges in green technology, nanoscience, and environmental stewardship.

Research Overview

Our lab explores the molecular design of sustainable, adaptive materials inspired by nature. We focus on understanding sequence–structure–function relationships in complex coacervates to engineer programmable soft materials for catalysis, energy storage membranes, coatings, and separations.

Bio-Inspired Coacervate Materials

We design stimuli-responsive protein–nucleic acid coacervates with tunable properties. Our work links molecular sequence design to phase behavior and function, enabling adaptive materials with controlled microenvironments and responsive transitions.

PhD Research: Reticular Chemistry

My doctoral work focused on reticular chemistry, building porous frameworks with precise connectivity and tunable host–guest interactions. These principles inform our approach to designing coacervate architectures with controlled properties.

Postdoctoral Work: Environmental Chemistry

My research also includes nanoparticle delivery systems for sustainable agriculture, emphasizing environmentally responsible nutrient delivery with advanced materials. This work connects materials design to practical environmental applications.
