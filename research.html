Research Overview

Our lab studies the molecular design of sustainable, adaptive materials inspired by nature. We integrate physical chemistry, spectroscopy, and materials science to address challenges in catalysis, energy storage, and environmental sustainability.

Bio-Inspired Coacervate Materials

We engineer stimuli-responsive protein–nucleic acid coacervates by understanding sequence–structure–function relationships. These soft materials enable controlled microenvironments and adaptive phase behavior, with applications in catalysis, separations, coatings, and energy storage membranes.

Reticular Chemistry

My doctoral research focused on reticular chemistry, designing porous molecular frameworks with precise connectivity and tunable host–guest interactions. This foundation informs our approach to controlling coacervate architecture at the molecular level.

Environmental Chemistry

I have developed nanoparticle delivery systems for sustainable agriculture, including environmentally benign foliar nutrient carriers. This experience highlights my commitment to applying materials design to real-world environmental challenges.

Methods & Tools

Our work combines advanced synthetic strategies with state-of-the-art characterization techniques, including AFM-Raman spectroscopy, TOF-SIMS, NMR, optical tweezers microrheology, and cryo-EM. These tools enable us to probe materials across length scales and connect molecular design to function.
