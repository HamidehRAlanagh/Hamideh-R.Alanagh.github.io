Reticular Chemistry

My doctoral research was rooted in reticular chemistry—the design and synthesis of porous crystalline frameworks built from strong bonds between molecular building blocks. This approach enabled the creation of functional materials with precise connectivity, tailored porosity, and tunable host–guest interactions. These principles continue to shape my thinking as I develop new classes of soft materials with programmable behavior.

Engineering MOFs for Biomedicine

  • I investigated the intracellular delivery of Cytochrome C using MOFs to trigger apoptosis in cancer cells, leveraging the porous structure for efficient protein encapsulation and release (unpublished data).
  • As a visiting scholar at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign, I developed the first MOF-based theranostic stent, combining therapeutic release with diagnostic imaging functionalities.
  • In another project, I studied the photoacoustic and fluorescence properties of MOFs in interaction with biliverdin, using spectroscopy and DFT calculations to reveal electron transfer mechanisms.

Controlled Molecular Transport with COFs

  • I achieved zero-order release of oxytocin through crystalline COF membranes, demonstrating how pore ordering and crystallinity can control release kinetics.
  • By tuning the atomic-level architecture of COFs, we were able to influence diffusion, stability, and molecular retention, especially important for biologically active compounds.

Porous Frameworks for Polymer Engineering

  • I synthesized metal-coordinated polymers using water as a solvent, replacing conventional organic synthesis routes.
  • These polymers were integrated into epoxy matrices to improve mechanical strength and tribological performance, enabling real-world applications in coatings and structural composites.
  • Further, by embedding polymers in MOF matrices, we observed enhanced crystallization behavior and shape-memory effects, confirmed via solid-state NMR and mechanical testing.

Catalysis, Diffusion, and Functional Insight

  • The role of pore size was shown to influence protein stabilization and apoptosome formation.
  • I explored how atomically smooth COF channels accelerated diffusion, offering models for designing next-generation delivery platforms.
  • We also designed low-dimensional porous biomineralized frameworks that acted as heterogeneous catalysts for CO2 reduction, addressing both energy and environmental challenges.

Global Collaborations in Framework Materials

  • Biomaterials and biointerfaces
  • Organic solvent nanofiltration (OSN) membranes
  • Advanced supercapacitor materials
  • Chiral framework systems
  • CO2 capture and conversion strategies

My background in reticular chemistry informs my current work on protein coacervates and soft matter architectures, where I continue to apply principles of modularity, tunable interactions, and structural hierarchy to engineer responsive, life-inspired materials.
