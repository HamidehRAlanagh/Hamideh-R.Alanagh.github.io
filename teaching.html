Teaching Philosophy

I believe effective teaching is grounded in clarity, curiosity, and inclusivity. My goal is to make complex topics accessible and relevant by linking theory to real-world applications. I emphasize student-centered learning that fosters critical thinking and creativity in chemistry and materials science.

Courses and Topics

I am prepared to teach foundational and advanced courses in:

  • Physical Chemistry (Thermodynamics, Kinetics, Spectroscopy)
  • Inorganic Chemistry and Materials Synthesis
  • Soft Matter and Biomaterials
  • Sustainable Materials Design and Green Chemistry
  • Characterization Techniques (AFM-Raman, NMR, TOF-SIMS, Microrheology)

Mentorship Approach

My mentorship emphasizes respect, communication, and individualized support. I aim to create an inclusive environment where students from diverse backgrounds feel welcome and empowered to ask questions, develop skills, and pursue their goals. I prioritize well-being alongside research excellence to foster resilient, confident scientists.
