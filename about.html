About Me

I am an interdisciplinary scientist focused on the molecular design of sustainable materials. My work integrates physical chemistry, biomaterials, and environmental chemistry to develop adaptive systems inspired by nature’s own strategies.

My research explores the sequence–structure–function relationships in complex coacervates, aiming to create tunable, stimuli-responsive materials for catalysis, energy storage, separations, and coatings.

Academic Journey

I completed a PhD specializing in reticular chemistry, developing porous frameworks with designed connectivity and tunable host–guest interactions. My postdoctoral work transitioned to soft matter science, investigating protein–nucleic acid coacervates, hierarchical materials, and environmentally responsible delivery systems for agriculture.

This background supports a holistic approach to materials design, bridging experimental physical chemistry, biophysical characterization, and applications in green technology.

Research Vision

My long-term vision is to advance sustainable materials science through deep understanding of molecular interactions and phase behavior. I am committed to training students in advanced spectroscopy and nanoscience, fostering interdisciplinary collaboration, and developing environmentally responsible solutions.
