Future Research Directions

My long-term vision is to establish a research program that positions the Nature × Matter Lab as a hub for innovation in stimuli-responsive coacervate materials. Inspired by the elegant complexity of nature’s molecular assemblies, our work seeks to address urgent sustainability challenges in chemistry and materials science through the design of multifunctional, adaptable soft matter systems.

With the recent Nobel Prize in Chemistry recognizing breakthroughs in protein structure prediction and design, the importance of understanding and programming biomolecular interactions has never been clearer. My future work builds on this foundation—merging molecular designing, spectroscopy, and chemical biology to pioneer sustainable materials and advance the frontiers of experimental physical chemistry.

These nature-inspired phase-separated systems offer a powerful framework to reimagine synthetic cells, develop responsive surfaces, and create programmable compartments for catalysis, delivery, and separation technologies. The transition toward more sustainable solutions requires us to mimic how nature organizes, adapts, and optimizes at the molecular level—and coacervates are ideal candidates for this purpose.
